Answers to common questions about the Indian Village Community Association and our neighborhood.

---

**Is the IVCA a mandatory HOA?**

No. The IVCA is a **voluntary** neighborhood association. A community survey conducted in 2022-2023 with 155 verified respondents (out of 495 properties) confirmed the community's preference for a voluntary association. Membership and dues are optional.

---

**How do I join or pay dues?**

Dues are voluntary and help fund maintenance of the entrance monuments, lighting, and community projects. To pay dues or learn more, [email the board](mailto:board@indianvillagefw.org).

---

**What do my dues pay for?**

The IVCA's primary expenses are entrance monument lighting (~$20/month) and community improvement projects like the entrance wall restoration. Monthly financial reports are published on the [Financial Reports](/financial-reports/) page for full transparency.

---

**Who is on the board?**

The current Board of Governors is listed on the [About Us](/about/) page. Board meetings are open to all Indian Village residents.

---

**What are the covenants / CC&Rs?**

Indian Village was developed across seven separate subdivisions between 1927 and 1957, each with its own set of recorded covenants. These historical covenants, along with later filings, are available on the [Documents](/documents/) page. For background on the legal history, see our [Survey and Voluntary Association](/posts/2023-01-22-survey-and-voluntary-association/) post.

---

**Is Indian Village on the National Register of Historic Places?**

Yes. The Indian Village Historic District was listed on the National Register in December 2009. The district encompasses 481 contributing buildings and is significant as a 20th-century automobile suburb. Learn more on the [History](/history/) page.

---

**Where is the neighborhood boundary?**

Indian Village is bounded roughly by Nuttman Avenue to the north, Bluffton Road to the east, Engle Road to the south, and the Norfolk Southern railroad right-of-way to the west. See the [Neighborhood Map](/map/) page or the [Assessor's Map](https://archive.ivcafw.org/Plat%2C%20Parcel%2C%20and%20Map%20Information/Assessors%20Map%20of%20Indian%20Village%20and%20Indian%20Hills.png) for details.

---

**How do I report a problem to the city?**

Use **Fort Wayne 311** for non-emergency city services like potholes, streetlights, code enforcement, trash, and more:
- Online: [fortwayne.thesmartcity311.com](https://fortwayne.thesmartcity311.com/)
- Phone: dial **311** from a Fort Wayne number

For police and fire, see the [Public Safety](/public-safety/) page.

---

**How do I report a blocked railroad crossing?**

Use the FRA's online tool at [fra.dot.gov/blockedcrossings](https://www.fra.dot.gov/blockedcrossings/) or call the Norfolk Southern Railroad Police at [800-453-2530](tel:8004532530).

---

**What school serves Indian Village?**

[Indian Village Elementary School](https://www.fwcs.k12.in.us/indianvillage) (Fort Wayne Community Schools) is located at 3835 Wenonah Lane, within the neighborhood. Middle school students attend Kekionga Middle School.

---

**Is there a community pool?**

Yes. [Pocahontas Swim Club (Poco Pool)](https://pocopool.org/) is located at 3020 Ojibway Trail, within the neighborhood. It's Fort Wayne's original swim club, open since 1961. The pool is open daily 12:30-8:30 from Memorial Day through Labor Day. Memberships are available on their website.
